Penguado Cookie Policy

Effective Date: August 10, 2025
Last Updated: October 31, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Penguado (“we,” “our,” “us”) uses cookies and similar technologies when you visit or use our website and services.


1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, keep you logged in, and improve your browsing experience.

Penguado also uses local storage, a similar technology stored in your browser, to remember login details securely.


2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies and local storage for the following purposes:

  • Authentication & Security — To store your UUID and email address so you remain logged in and your session is secure
  • Website Functionality — To ensure our services run smoothly and remember your settings
  • Analytics — To understand how users interact with Penguado using Google Analytics, which helps us improve features and user experience


3. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies: These are necessary for Penguado to function. They enable core features like login sessions and secure access.


4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies are set by third parties, such as Github Pages.
We encourage you to view their Privacy Policy.


5. Managing Cookies

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Please note:

  • Disabling essential cookies may prevent Penguado from functioning correctly
  • Disabling third party cookies will not affect our servers directly, but they may cause inconveniences such as long page loading times, and less security. Third party tracking cookies are not allowed on Penguado and user data is never sold.


6. Changes to This Policy


We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted here with a new “Last Updated” date.
